# Certification roadmap

Certifications are checkpoints, not a substitute for practical evidence.

## Stage 1: Networking foundation

- Complete free, structured networking study.
- Build a network diagram and packet-analysis project.
- Take an entry networking exam only if target jobs request it or a quick external checkpoint is useful.

## Stage 2: Core cybersecurity signal

- Prepare for CompTIA Security+ after networking, operating systems, and cybersecurity foundations are understood.
- Map each exam domain to a repository artifact.
- Do not schedule the exam until practice results and lab explanations are consistently strong.

## Stage 3: Practical blue-team evidence

- Complete Mission 001 and a mini-SOC project.
- Consider Blue Team Level 1 for practical phishing, forensics, threat-intelligence, SIEM, and incident-response work.

## Stage 4: Employer-driven specialization

Review at least 20 real target postings before purchasing a specialized exam.

- DFIR: prioritize employer-funded vendor training when a role names its forensic platform.
- SOC and detection: consider an analyst or SIEM credential when it repeatedly appears in target postings.
- OT: begin with official CISA ICS training and a safe OT lab before an expensive OT credential.
- AI governance: consider AIGP after responsible-AI and data-policy coursework, preferably with employer funding.

## Credentials intentionally deferred

- A+ unless an IT support route becomes necessary
- PenTest+ or advanced offensive credentials before defensive foundations
- expensive GIAC or vendor programs without employer funding
- low-recognition AI completion certificates used only as resume decoration

## Historical training

Prior law-enforcement, fire, EMT, tactical, investigative, and electrical credentials should be described as historical professional training unless their active status is independently confirmed.
